Che-Yu Chen

Hi! I'm currently a Postdoctoral Staff Scientist at the Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory. My primary research interest is the earliest stages of star formation, with a focus on the roles played by turbulence and magnetic fields at multiple scales, from supporting molecular clouds against gravitational collapse to regulating the formation of protostellar systems.

Here are some of my recent work.

I am a well-trained theoretical astrophysicist with extensive experiences on observational approaches. Though most of my work is theoretical study, I work closely with observers on both magnetic (polarimetric) and dynamic (spectral) properties in star-forming regions.

Formation of Magnetized Star-forming Cores in Shock-compressed Regions

I ran 3D MHD simulations with Athena to directly assess the role of magnetic fields in core formation, with convergent supersonic flows compressing turbulent gas to form dense, star-forming clumps in the post-shock regions (Chen & Ostriker 2014, 2015). A thorough statistical investigation on the properties and dynamic features of these pre-collapse cores is discussed in Chen & Ostriker (2018).

Dynamics in Star-forming Cores: a GBT-Argus Survey (DiSCo GAS)

I am currently leading a large observational program to survey detailed dynamics of dense cores in nearby clouds using the recently-commissioned Argus focal plane array on GBT. The complete survey (> 200 hr with 108 targets) will establish a statistical sample of core/envelope velocity structure. Our pilot study is published in Chen et al. (2019b). Also see the survey webpage: DiSCo GAS.

Gravitational Potential Identification of Cores (GRID-core)

   GRID-core is a core-finding method using the contours of the local gravitational potential to identify core boundaries, as described in Gong & Ostriker (2011). This method can be applied to both observed 2D surface density and simulated 3D volume density. Detailed discussion and user's manual can be found on Prof. Eve Ostriker's website. I have developed a modified 3D version of the GRID-core script that includes measurements of core's magnetic field and angular momentum. This function was utilized in my work Chen & Ostriker (2014), (2015), (2018). The complete script will soon to be made available here.

   An updated 2D version of the GRID-core algorithm in IDL is avilable here. This script is suitable for identifying clumps/cores in observed column density maps. The required input is a two-dimensional FITS file containing a map of the column density of atomic hydrogen (e.g. the figure on the top left). The code will generate two fits files containing information of identified cores, as well as a map illustrating the gravitational potential contours and the identified cores (e.g. the figure on the bottom left). A sample FITS map is provided here for code-testing purpose.
